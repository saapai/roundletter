# Breakfast Studio (wearebreakfast.com)

## Strongest Design Idea
Breakfast ingredients (egg, banana, raspberries, tape, switches, hands) are not decoration — they are the layout grid itself, "remixing" the page the way the studio remixes trailer music.

## Layout Pattern
Asymmetric vertical-stacked parallax composition. Primary content runs single-column, but absolutely-positioned floating objects pin to specific scroll depths and define the rhythm. The word "WORK" repeats horizontally across the viewport as a wide, banner-like type rail.

## Motion / Interaction
- Multi-depth parallax on the floating ingredient illustrations (each object scrolls at its own rate).
- Continuous idle motion on the egg balloon and diver — small loops that run independently of scroll so the page feels "alive" even when stationary.
- Scroll-revealed "WORK" text blocks fade/translate in as wide horizontal rails.
- A "Hungry for more" sticker acts as a persistent hover-lift focal point.
- Poster grid uses hover image swaps (project thumbnail → still frame).

## Mobile Experience
- Most decorative floating elements are dropped or simplified — keeping all of them would suffocate a small viewport.
- Repeating "WORK" rails collapse from horizontal banners into stacked single tokens.
- Poster grid converts to a single-column carousel.
- Idle animations are kept (cheap, charming) but parallax depth is reduced.

## Typography
- Bold, all-caps sans-serif headlines with generous, almost decorative letter-spacing.
- Body is also all-caps but smaller — the brand voice is a single tone of voice across scales.
- Hierarchy is purely scale-driven; one family, many sizes.
- Tight kerning on display, looser on body — inversion of the usual rule, which gives display type a stamped/pressed feel.

## Color
- Warm breakfast palette: egg-yolk yellow, soft orange, cream/white, sky-gradient blue.
- High contrast between dark text and light backgrounds for readability.
- Mood: optimistic, tactile, analog — the opposite of slick agency black-on-black.

## Genuinely Novel
Thematic illustrations as structural scaffold. Most sites layer brand art *over* a layout; here the layout is *defined by* the brand art — banana sets the column edge, switch sets the section break, tape marks the gallery boundary. Metaphor becomes layout logic.

## PORTABLE TO ROUNDLETTER
- Pick one or two recurring symbolic objects from the money/portfolio domain (a coin, a sealed envelope, a stamped letter, a candlestick) and use them as scroll-depth anchors that *define* sections rather than decorating them.
- Idle micro-animations decoupled from scroll (a coin slowly spinning, an envelope flap breathing) make the page feel inhabited without demanding interaction.
- Repeating wide horizontal type rails (e.g. "PUBLIC · PUBLIC · PUBLIC") between sections create a rhythmic break that's cheaper and more memorable than divider lines.
- One typeface, many sizes, all caps for both display and body — radical typographic restraint reads as brand confidence.
