## What I expect to go wrong

Four failure modes, ranked. If the mini-game ends with a regret text to the venue, it ends in one of these four ways.

1. **The pure-plays dilute me into the ground.** Three small-cap quantum names (IONQ, RGTI, QBTS) raise capital by issuing shares. Revenue can 10× while the stock halves. *Rule*: 50% same-day cut on any S-3 shelf, no averaging down on dilutive small-caps.

2. **Art doesn't clear.** Twelve pieces, starting bids $1–5, auction by SMS. If round-1 underclears, the art book sits at $49 and contributes nothing. *Mitigation*: each piece is independently priced. One sale is a signal; ten is a market.

3. **The bot bleeds the prediction book.** Polytrader trades live tennis + soccer (NBA shadow only). At STAKE_SCALE=0.15 the daily-loss kill is $2.84 and per-trade max is ~$1. *If it bleeds*, the kill switch is one env var; one-day loss caps before noon.

4. **I get bored, blink, and accept a regular trade.** Not a market risk; an operator risk. *Mitigation*: every trade is on the page before it clears. The boredom-trade is the one I can't take in private.
