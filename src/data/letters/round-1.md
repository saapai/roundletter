# Round 1 — What the Attention Built

*Filed twenty-five days in. The number is up. The products are real. The odds have not changed.*

The account opened at **$3,453.83** on April 12. The book sealed April 14 — ten quantum-computing positions locked, five AI agents arguing every move in public. Today the portfolio reads **~$4,300**. That is **+25% in twenty-five days**, which sounds like a story until you remember the story needs to be +2,800% by June 21.

I am 4.3% of the way to $100,000. The S&P is up about 3% over the same window. Annualized, this rate is **~2,500%** — roughly 250 times the S&P's annual return and 25 times what the best hedge funds have ever printed in a single year. That number is absurd. It is also the actual number. It will not sustain. The question was never whether it sustains — it was whether it compounds long enough to matter before it mean-reverts. The outperformance is real and it is not enough and those two facts will coexist for the entire duration of this project.

## The products

The pre-mortem described four rooms. What I did not describe is what the rooms would *build*. Twenty-five days later:

**Green Credit** — a prediction market on this portfolio. You can now bet on the bet. The instrument exists because someone asked "what are the odds?" and I realized the honest answer was a tradeable contract, not a number I made up. If the portfolio hits $100K, Green Credit pays. If it does not, Green Credit zeroes. The spread between my 8% estimate and whatever the market prices is the gap between my conviction and everyone else's. That gap is information. Publishing it is the product.

**The site itself** — 37 pages. None of them were planned. A prediction market, a Polymarket bot ($200 book), a Kalshi book ($50), 12 original artworks, 5 agent debates archived, and whatever this letter is. The homepage thesis was "attention is the upstream capital." Twenty-five days of evidence: every product on this site exists because someone paid attention to the portfolio, and the portfolio exists because someone paid attention to the site. The flywheel is small. It is also real.

**The investment pool** — two investors at $50 each, plus $61 in easter-egg gifted equity. $161 of other people's money touching a project that started as a birthday party dare. Not venture-scale. Not nothing.

## The Spirit Air parallel

This week, Spirit Airlines shut down. Seven hundred thousand strangers pledged $700 million to buy it back. The site crashed from traffic. LetsBuySpiritAir is not a serious acquisition vehicle. It is a proof of concept for attention as currency — a dead airline worth more as a meme than it was as a going concern, because 700K people deciding to care about the same thing at the same time is worth something even if what they care about is a bankrupt budget carrier with bad legroom.

I am not comparing my portfolio to a $700M crowdfund. I am pointing at the mechanism. The mechanism is: attention aggregates into capital before the capital exists. The capital is downstream. The crowd is upstream. The crowd showed up for Spirit because the story was absurd enough to be worth telling. The same mechanism, at a scale of dozens instead of hundreds of thousands, is why this site has products on it three weeks after launch. [[macro: The Spirit parallel is also a cautionary tale. Attention spikes decay. The 700K number will be 70K by next month and 7K by August. If your thesis is "attention is upstream," your follow-up thesis had better be "retention is the moat," and you do not have a retention thesis yet.]]

## What has not changed

The pre-mortem said 8% probability. I still think that is roughly right. The portfolio being up 25% in three weeks does not update the base rate because the base rate was never about the first three weeks. It was about sustaining a 29x over ten. Three weeks of green does not make a trend. It makes a sample size of one that happens to be positive.

Five agents argue every trade. They run on local LLMs — Ollama on an M5 Max, 128 GB, zero API cost. The memory system that feeds them, "Entrenched Coils," prioritizes contradictions over confirmations — it is, formally, an anti-echo-chamber, and it became a standalone research paper this month. The research paper exists because the portfolio exists. The portfolio exists because the site exists. The site exists because I told people about a birthday party. Upstream.

The party moved to **June 19** — a Friday, two days before the actual birthday. Sixty-three days left on the original clock. Forty-three days left on the party clock.
